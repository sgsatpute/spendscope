# GTM — Go To Market

## Exact target user

**Primary**: Engineering Manager or VP Engineering at a Series A–B startup (15–150 employees, $5M–$50M raised), who owns the annual engineering budget. They're on a cost-review cycle — either just got asked by their CFO to cut spend, or are preparing for a board meeting and need to show they're running lean. They have 3–8 developers paying for multiple AI tools, some of which were adopted tool-by-tool without central review.

**Secondary**: Founder/CTO at a 2–10 person pre-seed startup who just started getting surprised by their AI bills and has no finance team to help audit them.

The common thread: they're the person who would feel slightly embarrassed if their CFO asked "why are you paying for both Copilot and Cursor for the same developer?"

## First 100 users in 30 days — zero paid budget

1. **Day 1 — Post on HN "Show HN"**: "Show HN: I built a free AI tool spend auditor for startups." The tool needs to be live and fast. HN converts if the tool actually works. Target: 50–200 visitors. 5–15 emails captured. This alone might get to 50+ users.

2. **Days 2–7 — DM 30 engineering managers on X**: Search "engineering manager" + "Cursor" or "Copilot" in recent posts. DM: "Built a free tool that audits AI tool spend — took me 5 minutes and found $X/mo I was overpaying. Thought you'd find it useful: [link]." Not a pitch. Not a CTA. Just a relevant tool for someone who was already talking about it.

3. **Days 5–10 — Post in 3 targeted Slack communities**: Rands Leadership Slack (#tools channel), Indie Hackers Slack (#tools), SaaStr community. One message per community, conversational: "Been auditing my startup's AI spend — realized we were paying for Copilot Business and Cursor Pro for the same engineers. Built a free tool to check if others have the same problem: [link]"

4. **Days 7–14 — Pitch The Pragmatic Engineer or TLDR Tech for a link**: These newsletters have 500k+ EM subscribers. A genuine tool that solves a real problem gets picked up. Email the newsletter with a pitch: "You wrote about the AI tool cost explosion last month. I built a free auditor that benchmarks spend — happy to share it, no ask."

5. **Days 10–20 — X thread**: "We just audited our AI tool stack. Found $8,400/year we were leaving on the table. Here's what we found and what we changed: [thread]" End with a link. Threads with specific numbers and a story format get reshared.

6. **Ongoing — SEO via the shareable audit URLs**: Each public audit URL at `/audit/[id]` is indexed. As users share audits, they create long-tail SEO for terms like "Cursor vs Copilot cost comparison" and "AI tool spend audit."
